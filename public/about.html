About Me

Being a first generation from immigrant parents I was led to believe going to college, getting a degree and office job was the only future for myself. Afraid of disappointing my parents I attempted to go to community college in hopes of finding a career I wanted to pursue and eventually transfering to a University to get a bachelor's degree. Frustrated with what I wanted to do for my life long career, I was uncertain on what to pick for longer than most people my age. Becoming a dental hygienist seemed like a fit for me but when I took my first organic/inorganic chemistry class I knew the career I wanted to pursue was not going to go smoothly.

Fast forward two years I stopped joing to community college and started working in the mean time. I recently moved out of my parents house and wanted to learn how to make enough money where I did not have to worry about bills. This is when I finally decided to try how to learn to code! Like most people who have an ambition of wanting to become successful, I did not know where to start. Friends and family would tell me that I would need to go to school and get a degree to become successful in today's society. I refused to believe a degree would determine whether or not I become successful.

Nevertheless, I wanted to choose a language that seemed simple enough for me to my coding career. That language was HTML and soon after CSS. While learning one language at a time is what most people advise, HTML and CSS is an exception because they work hand to hand in creating something great! Javascript on the other hand was a whole new ballpark. Even though Javascript is a key language in front end devleopment, it is without a doubt a language that needs to be studied and practiced after learning HTML and CSS. Learning HTML, CSS and Javascript would be too overwhelming ultimately leading the individual to stop pursuing how to code.

I am slowly learning the ropes in front end web development in hopes of becoming a full stack developer so I can financially support myself and enjoy my 20s!
